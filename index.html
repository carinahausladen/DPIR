Carina I. Hausladen

University of Konstanz  ·  Causal & Experimental Methods  ·  Politics & Social Policy

I develop and apply causal, experimental, and quasi-experimental methods to core questions at the intersection of politics and social policy — from democratic participation and institutional design to labour market discrimination, behavioural compliance, and policy evaluation. I combine field and lab experiments, observational causal inference, and computational methods. Alongside substantive research I lead large-scale reproducibility initiatives spanning over 100 papers in top political science and economics journals.

Research programme

Methods leadership

Causal inference, experimental design, and quasi-experimental identification applied to political and social questions. I lead international mass-reproducibility initiatives (Nature, 2026) and develop computational tools — including machine learning and inverse reinforcement learning — for behavioural social science.

Democratic participation & institutions

How institutional design — voting rules, participatory budgeting, digital deliberation — shapes political legitimacy, fairness, and collective welfare. Empirical work with national platforms and Swiss municipalities, bridging experimental political science and real-world policy implementation.

Social policy & inequality

Causal evidence on labour market discrimination, behavioural compliance, and social interventions. Meta-analytic and field-experimental work — combining audit studies, chat data, and computational text analysis — with direct implications for anti-discrimination, public health, and governance policy.
